Machine Learning Fundamentals

Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that focuses on building systems that can learn from and make decisions based on data.

History:
The term was coined by Arthur Samuel in 1959. Early ML algorithms were based on symbolic logic, while modern ML is heavily dominated by statistical methods and neural networks.

Types of Machine Learning:
1. Supervised Learning: The model is trained on labeled data.
2. Unsupervised Learning: The model finds hidden patterns in unlabeled data.
3. Reinforcement Learning: The model learns by interacting with an environment to maximize a reward.

Common Algorithms:
- Linear Regression
- Decision Trees
- K-Means Clustering
- Support Vector Machines (SVM)

Impact:
ML is used today in everything from email spam filters to self-driving cars and medical diagnosis.
